Dataset: Alberta Wildfire Data (1996-2005)

Our AI system is trained on a rich dataset of historical wildfire incidents from Alberta, Canada, covering the period from 1996 to 2005. This dataset provides a comprehensive record of fire events, including key attributes like location, final size, start and end dates, and contributing factors. This historical data is crucial for our model to learn the complex patterns of wildfire behavior in the region.

Data Highlights

  • 10 years of comprehensive data
  • Geospatial information for precise location
  • Detailed fire duration and size metrics

Prediction Algorithm

The heart of WildWatchmen is a machine learning algorithm engineered to predict wildfire size and risk. By analyzing historical weather patterns, vegetation types, topography, and past fire behavior, our model generates probabilistic forecasts. This predictive power allows for proactive resource management and targeted mitigation strategies, ultimately helping to protect communities and natural landscapes.

Our Development Process: CRISP-DM

We adhere to the Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining (CRISP-DM), a structured and proven methodology for data science projects. This ensures our development process is rigorous, transparent, and focused on delivering reliable results.

1. Business Understanding

Defining the project objectives and requirements from a business perspective.

2. Data Understanding

Initial data collection and familiarization to identify data quality problems.

3. Data Preparation

Cleaning, transforming, and formatting the raw data for modeling.

4. Modeling

Selecting and applying various modeling techniques to the data.

5. Evaluation

Assessing the model's performance and quality before deployment.

6. Deployment

Integrating the model into our production systems for operational use.

Transparency and Limitations

We are committed to full transparency. While our AI provides powerful insights, it's essential to understand its limitations. Predictions are based on historical data and may not always capture the full complexity of real-world wildfire dynamics. We are constantly working to refine our models and improve accuracy.
